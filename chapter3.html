Chapter 3: The Last Gift

The owl returned, glowing in the soft moonlight. “You have done well,” he said. “You now understand: intelligence without kindness is hollow. True wisdom comes from the heart.” Tara’s mind now felt calm, light, and joyful. Her wish had transformed into something better — not knowing everything, but understanding what truly matters.

“You may now make a final wish,” said the owl, “one that will last forever.”
Tara thought for a moment. Then she said, “I wish every child who is kind can find this forest and learn what I did.” The owl smiled. “A beautiful wish.” A gust of wind carried golden leaves across the forest. From that day on, the Forest of Wishes became visible to all children who showed kindness — and it taught them that caring for others is the greatest knowledge of all.
